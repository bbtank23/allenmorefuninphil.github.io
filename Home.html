History of the Philippines

The Philippines is a country that is full of rich history. This section will talk about a brief history about the Philippines.

Pre-Colonial Philippines

Migrations from the Indonesian archipelago and other places are thought to have contributed to the settlement of the Philippine archipelago at least 30,000 years ago. Over the next millennia, there were other migrations. The widely dispersed islands saw a development and evolution of social and political order over time. The barrio was the fundamental societal unit (a Malay word for boat that came to be used to denote a communal settlement). There were large social divides within the barangay, including aristocrats, freemen, dependent and landless agricultural labourers, and slaves. Kinship groupings were governed by a datu (chief). Chinese traders joined the Indo-Malay migration over the years. The introduction of Islam by traders and missionaries to the Philippines was a significant event during the early era.

Spanish Philippines

The first European to be known to have set foot in the Philippines was Ferdinand Magellan. On his voyage around the world, he landed in March 1521. He attempted to claim territory for the Spanish king but was murdered by the local chief. After a number of more Spanish expeditions, Cebu's first permanent colony was founded in 1565. The Spanish established Manila as their capital in 1571 after overthrowing a local Muslim monarch, and they gave the colony King Philip II's name. By doing this, the Spanish hoped to increase their influence in the lucrative spice trade, establish closer ties with China and Japan, and convert people to Christianity. Only the third goal was ultimately accomplished. Church and state merged together to carry out Spanish goals, much like in other Spanish colonies. So Spain converted the locals

Philippine Revolt

José Rizal was a doctor, scientist, academic, and author. His works as a Propaganda Movement member—a group of intellectually engaged, upper-class Filipino reformers—had a significant influence on the emergence of the Filipino identity. His writings were outlawed, and he was forced into exile. In order to establish the Liga Filipina (Philippine League), a national, nonviolent political organization, Rizal returned from abroad in 1892. However, he was imprisoned and banished, and the league was disbanded. One outcome was the division of the nationalist movement between a more revolutionary and independence-minded plebeian constituency and the reform-minded ilustrados. Many of the latter joined the Katipunan, a covert organization dedicated to achieving national independence and established by Andres Bonifacio in 1892.

American Philippines

The First Philippine Republic and the United States engaged in an armed conflict that lasted from February 4, 1899, to July 2, 1902, known as the Philippine-American War[11] or the Filipino-American War (modern Filipino: Digmaang Pilipino-Amerikano), which the United States had previously referred to as the Philippine Insurrection or the Tagalog Insurgency. When the Spanish-American War came to an end in 1898, the United States refused to recognize the Philippines' declaration of independence and instead annexed the country under the terms of the Treaty of Paris. The conflict that followed can be seen as a continuation of the Philippine struggle for independence that started in 1896 with the Philippine Revolution against Spanish rule.

Martial Law

According to Marcos, martial law was the last line of defense against the rising unrest brought on by increasingly violent student protests, the alleged communist insurgency threats made by the newly formed Communist Party of the Philippines (CPP), and the Muslim separatist movement of the Moro National Liberation Front (MNLF). Arresting opposition members in Congress and the Constitutional Convention was one of his first moves. Except for Muslim regions in the south, where a separatist rebellion led by the MNLF erupted in 1973, the general public's first response to martial law was favorable. Thousands of soldiers and civilians continued to die in the revolt despite feeble attempts to negotiate a cease-fire. Upon the establishment of the National Democratic Front, the Communist insurgency grew.

Geography

The Philippines is an Archipelago with over 3 main regions with over 7,107 islands! In this section we're going to tackle the geography of the three main regions.

Area of the Philippines

The Philippines is a country that is located in the South East Asia region. It is an Archipelago that is made up of 7,107 island. It is part of the western pacific arc system so it is known to have active volaconoes! It is tropical country so make sure to bring clothes that aren't too hot!

Luzon

Luzon is the northern part of the Philippines. It is the main and biggest Island in the country thus making it the economic and political center of the country.

The island's north, which includes the majority of the Ilocos Region, is distinguished by a flat landscape that stretches east from the beach toward the Cordillera Central highlands.

The central section of Luzon is characterized by a flat terrain, known as the Central Luzon plain, the largest in the island in terms of land area. The Cagayan to the north, and the Pampanga to the south. In the middle of the plain rises the solitary Mount Arayat.

The Southern Luzon is dominated by the Laguna de Bay (Old Spanish, "Lake of Bay town"), the largest lake in the country. The 949-square-kilometre (366 sq mi) lake is drained into Manila Bay by the Pasig River, one of the most important rivers in the country due to its historical significance and because it runs through the center of Metro Manila.

Visayas

Visayas is one of the three main islands of the Philippines. Located in the central part of the archipelago, it consists of several islands, primarily surrounding the Visayan Sea

Central Visayas is a region that is made up of the island provinces of Bohol, Cebu, Negros Oriental, and Siquijor. Highlands and short coastal areas of fertile soil define the land

Beautiful natural landmarks including beaches, caverns, mountains, and lakes can be found across Eastern Visayas. Due to its plentiful economic resources, stunning natural surroundings, Eastern Visayas is becoming a popular travel and investment destination.

Western Visayas, home to the country's most renowned beach, Boracay. Western Visayas is a region that is located south of the Sibuyan sea and south west of the Visayan Sea. It is known for it's beaches and coastal areas. Very popular tourist attraction due to the famous Boracay beach that is known all over other countries, visited by many tourists.

Mindanao

Mindanao is the second-largest island in the Philippines. The island is mountainous, and is home to Mount Apo, the highest mountain in the country.

More than 60% of Northern Mindanao's total land area are classified as forest land. Its seas abound with fish and other marine products. The abundant vegetation, natural springs and high elevation contribute to the region's cool, mild and invigorating climate.

Central has, large valleys, mountain ranges, and coastlines can all be found nearby. The region is notable for its extensive river system, particularly in the Cotabato Basin, a huge depression surrounded on three sides by mountains.

The Southern region is characterized by extensive mountain ranges extending along the western border, in the northern central area and in the northwestern area leading to the peninsula in the southeast with uneven distribution of plateaus and lowlands. Its coastline reached to 1,600 square kilometer.

Demographics

There are over 100 million Filipinoes all over the Philippines, so it's no wonder that its very diverse! From Igorots from the north to Manobo from the south ranging from young to old, men or women.

Religion

Philippines is a pre-dominated Catholic country with over 79% Filipinoes being Catholic, 9% of Filipinoes belongs to Protestanism or other types of Christianity. The largest Christian religion beside Catholic is Iglesia Ni Cristo with over 2.6% Filipinoes. Even though the Philippines is a predominant Christian country there are also Muslims living in the southern part of the Philippines with over 6% of Filipinoes being Muslims.

Ethnicity

Philippines is a very diverse in ethnicity too! There are over 120 languages spoken by different ethnicities in the Philippines. Visayans which consists of Cebuano, Waray, Hiligayon/Illongo, Karay-a, Aklanon, Masbatenyo, and Romblomanon, with over 31.6% of Filipinoes. Tagalog people are the second major ethnicity in the Philippines, with over 28.1% Filipinoes being pure tagalog. There are also minor ethnicities, Illocanos consists of 9% Filipinoes, Bikolano people consisting of 6%, Kampapampangan people 3%, Pangasinense people consists of 2%, Zamboagueno 1.5%, & Others 23.3% people.

Culture

Philippine is an archipelago consisting of 7,107 islands, so don't be surprised that there are hundreds or thousands of different cultures all over the Philippines! In this section we are going to talk about the tribes, and festivals all over the country.

Tribes

Igorots

The Igorots may be loosely split into two subgroups: the bigger group, which is well-versed in rice terrace cultivation and resides in the south, central, and western regions, and the smaller group, which is located in the east and north. The peoples today referred to by the term did not see themselves as belonging to a single, cohesive ethnic group before the Spanish colonized the islands. The Igorot tribes consists of; Ifugaos, Bontoc, Kalinga, Isneg, Ikalahan, and many more!

Lumad

Indigenous Austronesian people known as the Lumad live in the southern Philippines. The word means "native" or "indigenous" in Cebuano. The term is an acronym for Katawhang Lumad, the official name of the Lumad Mindanao Peoples Federation (LMPF) founding assembly, which took place on June 26, 1986, in Balindog, Kidapawan, Cotabato, Philippines. The term was accepted in Philippine law when President Corazon Aquino signed Republic Act 6734 into law, where it was used in Article. To separate Lumad ethnic groupings from the islands of Mindanao.

Badjaos

They are referred to as the sea tribes that live on houseboats and are originally from the Sulu Islands in Mindanao. By relying on the water as divers, fisherman, and navigators, they attempt to make ends meet. The bulk of them have fled the region's hostilities and traveled to other nations like Malaysia and Indonesia, while those who remained in the Philippines have relocated to various parts of Luzon.

Festivals

Panagbenga Festival

Panagbenga Festival is a month-long annual flower occasion in Baguio. The term is of Kankanaey origin, meaning "season of blooming". The festival, held in February, was created as a tribute to the city's flowers and as a way to rise from the devastation of the 1990 Luzon earthquake

Bangus Festival

The Bangus Festival is a ten-day celebration taking place in Dagupan City every April. The festivities include an array of sports competitions, fairs, and street parties. And of course, the festival is full of culinary demonstrations, tastings, and contests.

Luzon

Numerous natural and cultural attractions may be found on Luzon. Here, the enjoyment is greater. Here is a list of places to visit to get you started. Note this. The majority of these have not been negatively impacted by mass tourism. We included both the best locations on Luzon and lesser-known tourist attractions in this guide.

Let's begin!

Baguio

In the northern part of the Philippines lies the 'Summer Capital of the Philippines' also known as Baguio City!

Burnham Park

Burnham Park! The main park of Baguio city, in here you can ride bikes, row boats, play soccer, explore new things, and many more! You'll also find rose gardens, children playgrounds, and traditional Igorot gardens in this park.

Wright Park

The park is named after Governor Luke E. Wright, an area for recreation and leisure. The Wright Park Riding Circle holds the main activity that people go to the park for horseback riding. In fact, Wright Park is often mistakenly called “Ride Park” especially by kids because of the riding.

Mines View

Mines View Park is one of the famous tourist spots across Baguio pack with shops selling snacks, trinkets, botanical items, and others. Picture taking services with traditional Igorot clothes, horses, dogs and more are also available towards the lower half of the park.

Camp John Hay

A former military facility, Camp John Hay draws golfers to the Jack Nicklaus-designed course at the Camp John Hay Golf Club, and the Baguio Country Club's greens and fairways. Pine trees line the Eco Trail, providing shade to joggers and hikers, Tree Top Adventure with ziplining courses.

Intramuros

In the heart of capital city of Manila lies the so called 'Walled City' of the Philippines, Intramuros!

Fort Santiago

Fort Santiago is the oldest Spanish bastion in the Philippines. Situated inside the walled city of Intramuros in Manila, the site witnessed many historical events of the Philippines. The exact spot where Fort Santiago now stands was once a Muslim kingdom ruled by a chieftain named Rajah Sulayman. When the Spaniards landed in the Philippines in 1571, they destroyed the site and built a fortress instead, naming it after Spain’s patron saint, Saint James (Santiago in Spanish). The structure served as their defense fortress.

Manila Cathedral

Manila Cathedral is recognized as the main Roman Catholic Church in the Philippines. Located in the Plaza de Roma, Intramuros, its official name is Manila Metropolitan Cathedral-Basilica and is the seat of the Archbishop of the Archdiocese of Manila. When Catholicism arrived in the Philippines in 1521, it brought with it the western church architecture. One of the most significant and magnificent churches built during the Spanish colonization is the Manila Cathedral. This church is only located at Intramuros!

Baluerte de San Diego

Baluarte de San Diego is a spade-shaped bulwark located inside the historic district of Intramuros in Manila City. This fort was created from the remains of the upper portion of a circular watch tower built from 1586-1587. Since it was erected, Baluarte de San Diego underwent a series of repairs and restorations after getting damaged during wars and an earthquake. The baluarte had been breached and fallen in 1762 during a battle with the British forces.

Casa Manila

Casa Manila is one of the most popular tourist spots in Intramuros. It’s opposite the San Agustin Church and is a replica of Spanish colonial mansion of 19th century Manila. It recreates the rich merchant’s way during the Spanish colonization, showcasing period furniture coming from Chinese, European, and Philippine origins. There are a total of 3 floors inside the area. On the ground floor, you’ll see the main entry gate called Puerta Principal. It is made of heavy wood.

Bicol

Known for the almost perfectly conical Mount Mayon Volcano and its panoramic views from Legazpi, Albay, Bicol comprises of church-laden historic routes, coconut-rich regional cuisine (with copious amounts of native chilies), spectacular fiestas and festivals, prime diving spots, virgin beaches of Catanduanes, and vibrant marine biodiversity ideal for spotting and snorkeling with the whale sharks of Donsol. That's why it's the perfect destination for countless itineraries – there's always something to do and places to explore!

Casagawa Ruins

In 1814, Mayon Volcano recorded its most violent eruption, completely burying the entire town of Cagsawa by fast-flowing volcanic matter, except for the belfry. But unlike any other beautiful place in the Philippines, this once lively town had to go through such tragedy for it to be one of the most popular Albay tourist spots in Daraga.. Set against the magnificent backdrop of its creator, Cagsawa Ruins Park is declared a national cultural treasure that brings visitors closer to the volcano. The site is just a short drive away from Daraga proper, and once you’ve arrived, several exhilarating Mayon Volcano tours await you, including the Mayon ATV tour around the foothills of Mount Mayon as well as awe-inspiring landscapes in the area.

Subic Beach

Kickstart your island getaway on Subic Beach, Matnog, one of the Philippines' hidden beach attractions solely for its blushing, pinkish-white sands against the clear turquoise waters. Subic Beach is an idyllic beach spot in Calintaan and one of the pink sand islands in Matnog (others being Tikling and the Juan Lagoon Fish Sanctuary). Offering more than just its stunning waves and finely crushed red corals decorating its beds of sand, Subic Beach is also the gateway to a series of sea caves and rock formations that you can explore from a short sailing distance away. Awe in amazement on it's clear sand and clear water, to add to that it's refreshing indeed!

Visayas

The Visayas are a major island and cultural grouping in the midsection of the Philippines. Nearly every part of the region has some fine beaches along its coasts, some fine diving areas including many coral reefs teeming with life, and some interesting historic buildings, mostly from the Spanish colonial period, in the cities and some towns. Most of the larger islands have mountains near the center, most of them volcanic in origin though few are active now. The country's best-known resort island, Boracay, is in the northwestern corner of the region. Diving destinations include Malapascua in Cebu Province, Panglao Island in Bohol Province, Apo Island in Negros Oriental, Danjugan Island in Negros Occidental, and many others. Other tourist draws include Metro Cebu which is the main urban center of the region, the Chocolate Hills of Carmen, Bohol, and whale watching in the Tañon Strait near Bais, Negros Oriental.

Boracay

Boracay Island, one of the top beaches in the Philippines and one of the best Visayas tourist spots, is blessed with a long stretch of powdery white sand beach (called White Beach), crystal clear and azure waters, a stunning sunset, and more. The island doesn’t fall short on adventures and activities for people of all ages.

You'll never run out of exciting things to do in Boracay, from swimming, island hopping in the Philippines, diving, snorkeling, parasailing, kayaking, dining, and parties. Because of these, Boracay has been cited multiple times as one of the best islands and beaches in the world according to reputable publications and travelers' choice lists.

Chocolate Hills

The island province of Bohol in the Philippines may be smaller than its Visayan neighbors Cebu and Negros which are home to some of the top tourist spots in Visayas and most beautiful islands in the Philippines, but it doesn't fall short of stunning sights and activities, such as its famous Chocolate Hills.

Joining Chocolate Hills tours will treat you to a breathtaking view of a thousand beautiful cone-shaped hills stretched over 50 square kilometers of land across the towns of Carmen, Batuan, and Sagbayan. This is the tourist spot that put Bohol on the map of top tourist destinations in the Philippines, and that has baffled many geologists.

Kawasan Falls

Kawasan Falls is the magnificent set of cascades that draw thousands of visitors to Cebu Island. Kawasan is one of the top Cebu tourist spots because of its lush surroundings and striking turquoise water.

Visitors from all over visit Kawasan to enjoy its raw beauty and serene landscape. It is a great spot for swimming as the water is remarkably clean and refreshing. The water that flows through the waterfalls is part of the Matutinao River System. This system is highly regarded for having one of the cleanest inland body of water in the Philippine Islands.

The waterfall has two main tiers, which feature natural pools perfect for swimming. The first tier stands at about 40 meters. The second tier is only around 20 meters and is often less visited because people need to trek for 10 minutes from the first cascade to reach it.

Mindanao

Being the second largest island group next to Luzon, Mindanao has been dubbed the country's “Land of Promise” because of its promising natural resources. It is also known worldwide for being home to the surf capital of the Philippines, a famous highly-urbanized city, and jaw-dropping beaches.

Enchanted River,

There is probably no other river in the country that will surpass the blueness of the famous Enchanted River, another mystic gem nestled in Hinatuan town in Surigao del Sur. It is 290 meters long, but its depth remains a mystery for many divers.

There were diving expeditions made in the past, yet there is no clear picture of what lies at the bottommost part of the mystical river. With water flowing to the Philippine Sea and the Pacific Ocean, the Hinatuan Enchanted River opens up to an estuary where salt water mixes with the river’s spring water.

The famous blue river is only a small portion of it. Divers even found an opening of an underwater cave at the river’s deepest portion. To preserve its beauty, visitors are not allowed to swim in the main pool. There is a designated area for swimming, which is only 10 meters away from the main pool.

Mount Apo

Towering over Southern Mindanao, Mount Apo is the highest mountain in the Philippines located in the Durian Capital of the country, Davao. It rises over 2,956 MASL and covers the provinces of Davao del Sur and North Cotabato.

Mount Apo features mossy forests, swamps, and volcanic structures. It possesses an array of lush landscapes that mountaineers can pass through by following the Kidapawan-Magpet Trail. When hiking Mount Apo, you must consider its difficulty level of 7/9 that requires a major climb.

Pink Mosque

Dubbed as the first-ever pink mosque in the Philippines, Masjid Dimaukom or The Pink Mosque is a Maguindanao centerpiece constructed in 2013 to become the new image and representation of the province. The sacred building is the initiative of Mayor Samsudin Dimaukom of the Datu Saudi Ampatuan town who happens to have chosen pink as his favorite color.

Pink represents peace, harmony, and love. Likewise, the people of Datu Saudi Ampatuan believe that the mosque will bring them unity and brotherhood. Inspired by the mosque’s color and design, the town’s municipal hall, police stations, and public market are also set to be repainted pink. When you enter the town, you will also be greeted by the pink flags lined on the sidewalks.
